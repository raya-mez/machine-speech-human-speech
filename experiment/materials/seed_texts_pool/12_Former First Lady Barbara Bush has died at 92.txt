Barbara Bush, the former first lady of the United States and matriarch of one of the country’s most prominent political families, has died at the age of 92. A family spokesperson confirmed that Bush passed away peacefully at her home in Houston, surrounded by loved ones.

Wife of the 41st president, George H.W. Bush, she served as first lady from 1989 to 1993. Known for her trademark white hair, sharp wit and outspoken advocacy for literacy, Bush championed education initiatives throughout her public life. She founded the Barbara Bush Foundation for Family Literacy in 1989, helping raise millions of dollars to promote reading and writing programs nationwide.

Born Barbara Pierce in New York City in 1925, she married George H.W. Bush in 1945 and raised six children, including former President George W. Bush and former Florida Governor Jeb Bush. She was widely regarded as a steadying force within the Bush family and a candid voice in American politics.

Tributes poured in from leaders across the political spectrum, praising her dedication to public service, family and country. Funeral arrangements are expected to be announced in the coming days. She will be remembered for her resilience and enduring grace throughout her life.
