Renowned quantum physicist Dr. Amelia Hart has been named Australian of the Year, honored for her pioneering research in quantum computing and her tireless advocacy for science education. Announced at a ceremony in Canberra on Tuesday night, the award recognizes Hart’s leadership in developing ultra-stable qubits that could dramatically accelerate the arrival of practical quantum technologies.

Hart, a professor at the University of Sydney and director of the National Quantum Innovation Centre, has spent two decades advancing methods to reduce error rates in quantum processors. Her team’s breakthrough last year demonstrated a scalable architecture capable of operating at room temperature, a milestone experts say could lower costs and expand commercial applications.

Beyond the laboratory, Hart has championed greater access to STEM education for girls and students in regional communities. She has launched mentorship programs, advised government on critical technology policy, and spoken widely about the ethical implications of quantum breakthroughs.

Prime Minister said Hart embodies “curiosity, collaboration and courage.” Accepting the award, Hart dedicated the honor to her students and colleagues, urging sustained investment in research to secure Australia’s place in the global quantum race. She called the moment a reminder of science’s power to unite nations through shared discovery.
