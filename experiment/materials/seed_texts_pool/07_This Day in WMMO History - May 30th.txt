In 1985, WMMO officially flipped its format to classic rock, a bold move that distinguished the station in a competitive market and set the tone for decades of legendary programming. The debut song that morning—The Rolling Stones’ “Start Me Up”—signaled a new era that resonated with Central Florida audiences.

Years later, on May 30, 1999, WMMO hosted its first annual Memorial Weekend Rock Marathon, a 48-hour, commercial-light tribute to rock’s greatest icons. The event quickly became a fan-favorite tradition, drawing record-breaking listenership and cementing the station’s reputation for immersive, fan-driven programming.

May 30th also marks a milestone from 2012, when WMMO launched its revamped mobile app, expanding its reach beyond terrestrial radio and connecting with a new generation of digital listeners. The app’s debut included exclusive backstage interviews and live concert streams, underscoring the station’s commitment to innovation.

From format-defining decisions to community-building broadcasts, May 30th stands as a testament to WMMO’s enduring dedication to classic rock and the loyal audience that continues to turn it up loud.
