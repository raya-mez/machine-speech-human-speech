Uber Technologies is facing a significant leadership shake-up following the release of an internal investigation into workplace misconduct and compliance failures, company officials confirmed Tuesday. The months-long probe, launched after a series of employee complaints and regulatory inquiries, uncovered lapses in oversight, inconsistent enforcement of company policies, and failures in reporting procedures.

As a result, three senior executives, including the head of regional operations, have stepped down, while two others have been placed on administrative leave pending further review. The company’s board said it has appointed an interim compliance chief and will form a new ethics committee to strengthen accountability measures.

Chief Executive Dara Khosrowshahi said in a statement that the findings were “deeply concerning” and pledged to implement sweeping reforms. “We are committed to rebuilding trust with our employees, drivers, riders, and regulators,” he said. “This moment demands transparency and decisive action.”

The investigation also prompted renewed scrutiny from transportation regulators in several major cities, where Uber’s operating licenses are subject to periodic review. Industry analysts say the shake-up could temporarily disrupt operations but may ultimately position the company for more stable growth if reforms are effectively implemented.

Uber shares closed down 3 percent following the announcement.
